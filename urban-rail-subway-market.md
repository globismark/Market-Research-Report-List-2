<p><p>Urban rail subway market types can be classified into four categories: A Type, B Type, C Type, and L Type. A Type subways typically have higher capacity and larger trains, making them suitable for densely populated urban areas. B Type subways are medium capacity and are commonly found in smaller cities or suburbs. C Type subways are light capacity and are typically seen in rural or low-density urban areas. L Type subways are light rail systems that often operate above ground and are used in both urban and suburban areas.</p></p>
