<p><strong>The Home Sleep Monitoring Equipment Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Contact</li><li>Contactless</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Contact home sleep monitoring equipment includes devices that require physical contact with the body to measure sleep patterns, such as wristbands or headbands. Contactless equipment, on the other hand, does not require direct contact with the body and may use sensors placed near the bed or on the nightstand to monitor sleep. Contact devices may provide more accurate data but can be uncomfortable for some users, while contactless devices offer convenience and ease of use. Both types play a crucial role in the home sleep monitoring equipment market, catering to different preferences and needs.</p></p>
