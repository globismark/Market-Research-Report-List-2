<p><p>Surrogacy services typically fall into two main types: gestational surrogacy and traditional surrogacy. Gestational surrogacy involves the implantation of an embryo created through in vitro fertilization, where the surrogate has no genetic relation to the child. Traditional surrogacy, on the other hand, involves artificial insemination where the surrogate's own egg is fertilized by the intended father's sperm. Each type of surrogacy has different legal and ethical considerations, as well as varying success rates and costs associated with the process.</p></p>
