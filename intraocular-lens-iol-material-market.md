<p><p>Intraocular Lens (IOL) materials are categorized into four main types: PolymethylMethacrylate (PMMA), Silicone, Hydrophilic Acrylic or Hydrogel, and Hydrophobic Acrylic. PMMA lenses are rigid and provide good optical quality. Silicone lenses are flexible and offer long-term stability. Hydrophilic Acrylic or Hydrogel lenses are soft and biocompatible, allowing for easy insertion. Hydrophobic Acrylic lenses have a low incidence of posterior capsule opacification. Each material has unique characteristics that cater to different patient needs and preferences in the IOL market.</p></p>
