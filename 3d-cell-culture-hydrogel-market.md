<p><p>Natural hydrogels are derived from natural sources such as collagen, gelatin, and alginate, closely mimicking the extracellular matrix in the body. They provide a more biocompatible environment for cells to grow and interact. Synthetic hydrogels, on the other hand, are created in a controlled laboratory setting, offering more customizable properties such as stiffness and degradation rate. Both types of hydrogels are used in 3D cell culture applications to provide structural support and mimic the natural cellular environment for various research and therapeutic purposes.</p></p>
