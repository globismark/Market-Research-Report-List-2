<p><p>NIB magnet market includes bonding and sintering types. Bonding refers to the process of assembling magnet powder into desired shapes using a binder material. This method is cost-effective and allows for complex shapes. Sintering involves compacting and heating magnet powder to form a solid state without melting. This method produces magnets with high magnetic properties but is more expensive. Both bonding and sintering are widely used in the NIB magnet market for various applications.</p></p>
