<p><p>Biconical dryers are commonly classified into two main types: atmospheric dryers and vacuum dryers. Atmospheric dryers operate at ambient pressure and use hot air to remove moisture from materials, making them suitable for a wide range of applications. Vacuum dryers, on the other hand, operate at reduced pressure levels, allowing for faster and more efficient drying processes. They are often used for heat-sensitive materials or when low moisture levels are required. Both types of dryers offer unique advantages depending on the specific drying requirements.</p></p>
