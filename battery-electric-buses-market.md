<p><p>Battery-Electric Buses come in three main market types - BEV (Battery Electric Vehicle), PHEV (Plug-in Hybrid Electric Vehicle), and FCEV (Fuel Cell Electric Vehicle). BEVs are powered solely by electricity, requiring frequent charging. PHEVs have a battery that can be charged through both electric outlets and by the internal combustion engine. FCEVs use hydrogen to produce electricity through a fuel cell. Each type has its own benefits and challenges, catering to different needs and infrastructure availability in the market.</p></p>
