<p><p>General Purpose Transistors are widely used in a variety of electronic devices and circuits. There are three main types of General Purpose Transistors based on their structure and functioning: NPN, NPN/PNP, and PNP. NPN transistors are most commonly used, where current flows from the collector to the emitter when a small current is applied to the base. PNP transistors operate in the opposite manner, with current flowing from the emitter to the collector. NPN/PNP transistors combine both NPN and PNP characteristics for versatility in circuit design.</p></p>
