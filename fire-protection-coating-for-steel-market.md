<p><p>Fire protection coatings for steel are commonly divided into two types: cellulose and hydrocarbon. On the other hand, hydrocarbon-based coatings are made from synthetic materials and offer enhanced fire resistance compared to cellulose coatings. Both types of coatings are designed to prevent steel structures from reaching their critical temperature during a fire, thus maintaining their structural integrity and safety.</p></p>
