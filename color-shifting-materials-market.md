<p><p>Color-shifting materials are used in a variety of markets for their unique ability to change color in response to external stimuli. Thermochromism materials change color with temperature fluctuations, while photochromism materials respond to light exposure. Electrochromism materials change color due to an electric field, solvatochromism materials change with solvent polarity, and cathodchromism materials change with cathodic potential. Other markets for color-shifting materials include piezochromism (pressure-induced color change), mechanochromism (mechanical stress-induced color change), and triboluminescence (friction-induced color change).</p></p>
